# Valhalla Legacy Group — Holdings Framework

## Investment Philosophy

Valhalla Legacy Group focuses on **real assets** with clear operational value and long-term appreciation potential. We prioritize:

1. **Industrial Real Estate** — Facilities that support manufacturing and production
2. **Land with Development Potential** — Parcels suitable for industrial, light manufacturing, or housing development
3. **Income-Producing Properties** — Assets that generate lease income while appreciating
4. **Strategic Holdings** — Properties that support the operating companies (Grid, Stax, SMB)

### Core Principles

- **Durable Assets** — Real estate with long-term value
- **Operational Synergy** — Assets that support the ecosystem
- **Risk Mitigation** — Diversification across asset types and phases
- **Long-Term Horizon** — Focus on generational wealth, not quick flips
- **Quiet Operations** — Minimal public presence, institutional approach

## Land Acquisition Criteria

### Target Parcels

**Size**: 3–5 acres (ideal for initial phases)  
**Zoning**: Light industrial, manufacturing, or mixed-use (flexible)  
**Location**: Inland Northwest region (North Idaho and surrounding areas)

### Essential Requirements

**Access**
- Good road access (not remote or difficult to reach)
- Suitable for truck traffic (modular transport, material delivery)
- Reasonable distance from major markets

**Utilities**
- Power availability (adequate capacity for manufacturing)
- Water availability (well or municipal)
- Septic potential or sewer access
- Internet/communications (for operations)

**Topography**
- Relatively flat or easily developable
- Good drainage
- Suitable for building construction

**Zoning & Entitlements**
- Permitted uses include manufacturing, light industrial
- Reasonable setback and coverage requirements
- No major environmental constraints

### Due Diligence Checklist

- Title work and survey
- Zoning verification
- Utility availability confirmation
- Environmental assessment (Phase I, if needed)
- Access verification
- Cost estimates for development
- Market analysis (if applicable)

## Development Phasing

### Phase 1: Land Acquisition

- Identify target parcels
- Due diligence
- Negotiate purchase
- Close on acquisition

### Phase 2: Basic Infrastructure

- Access road development
- Utility connections (power, water, septic/sewer)
- Basic site grading
- Drainage improvements
- Fencing (if needed)

### Phase 3: First Facility (Grid)

- 40×100 shop building
- Office space
- Material yard
- Parking
- Utilities to building
- Lease to Grid Cabinet Systems

### Phase 4: Stax Prototype Yard

- 2–3 covered production bays
- Crane infrastructure
- Dry storage
- Additional utilities
- Lease to Stax

### Phase 5: Full Modular Factory

- 12,000–20,000 sq ft facility
- High-bay construction
- Multiple production lines
- Expanded yard
- Lease to Stax

### Phase 6: Prototype Village

- Site development for 2–4 homes
- Utilities to each site
- Access roads
- Landscaping (minimal)
- Build Stax units as:
  - AirBnB rental
  - Model home
  - Spec home
  - Showroom

### Phase 7: Expansion

- Additional facilities as needed
- Spec home clusters
- Additional land acquisition (if needed)

## Relationship Model: Valhalla as Landlord

### Operating Companies as Tenants

**Grid Cabinet Systems**
- Leases shop facility
- Lease-to-own option (if structured)
- Pays market-rate or internal-rate rent

**Stax Modular**
- Leases prototype yard, then full factory
- Lease-to-own option (if structured)
- Pays market-rate or internal-rate rent

**Sugar Mountain Builders**
- May lease office space (if needed)
- Primarily service business (less facility-dependent)

### Lease Structure

**Initial Leases**
- Market-rate or internal transfer pricing
- Terms: 3–5 years (typical)
- Renewal options

**Lease-to-Own Options**
- Option to purchase facility at predetermined terms
- Builds equity for operating companies
- Maintains asset control for Valhalla until exercised

### Benefits of This Model

**For Operating Companies**
- Lower capital requirements (no land/building purchase upfront)
- Flexibility to scale
- Focus on operations, not real estate

**For Valhalla**
- Asset ownership and appreciation
- Steady lease income
- Control over development
- Long-term value creation

## Long-Term Hold vs. Exit Theory

### Long-Term Holds

**Industrial Facilities**
- Hold and lease to operating companies
- Generate steady income
- Appreciate over time
- Provide operational stability

**Spec Homes**
- Build and hold initially (rental income, AirBnB)
- Sell when market conditions are favorable
- Or: Hold long-term as rental portfolio

**Prototype Village**
- Hold as demonstration/showcase
- Generate rental income (AirBnB, model home)
- Marketing asset for SMB and Stax
- Potential sale of individual units

### Exit Strategies

**Facility Sales**
- Sell facilities to operating companies (lease-to-own)
- Or: Sell to third parties if operating companies don't exercise option

**Land Sales**
- Sell undeveloped land if no longer needed
- Or: Develop and sell improved parcels

**Portfolio Sale**
- Sell entire portfolio to investor or larger entity
- Or: Spin off individual assets

**Operating Company Sales**
- Operating companies can be sold independently
- Valhalla retains real estate
- New owners lease facilities from Valhalla (or purchase)

## Risk Mitigation

### Diversification

- Multiple operating companies (not dependent on one)
- Multiple revenue streams (leases, rentals, sales)
- Phased development (not all capital at once)

### Asset Protection

- Separate legal entities (operating companies separate from Valhalla)
- Proper insurance
- Clear lease agreements
- Professional management

### Market Risk

- Long-term horizon (less sensitive to short-term market fluctuations)
- Real assets (tangible value)
- Operational value (not just speculative)
