# Sales Strategies — Valhalla Ecosystem

This document outlines sales strategies for each operating company in the Valhalla ecosystem.

---

## Sugar Mountain Builders — Sales Strategy

### Lead Generation

**Primary Channels**

1. **Website & SEO**
   - Mountain-modern construction keywords
   - Local SEO (North Idaho, Inland Northwest)
   - Portfolio showcase
   - Blog content (mountain living, construction tips)

2. **Referrals**
   - Past clients
   - Real estate agents
   - Architects and designers
   - Subcontractors and suppliers

3. **Partnerships**
   - Stax modular inquiries (homeowners interested in modular)
   - Grid cabinet customers (remodel referrals)
   - Real estate developers
   - Land developers

4. **Direct Outreach**
   - Networking events
   - Home shows
   - Local business associations
   - Mountain/lake community events

5. **Portfolio Marketing**
   - Social media (Instagram, Facebook)
   - Before/after photos
   - Project case studies
   - Video tours

### Sales Process

**Inquiry → Consultation**

1. **Initial Contact**
   - Website form, phone, email
   - Quick response (within 24 hours)
   - Initial qualification (budget, timeline, location)

2. **Consultation Call**
   - Understand project goals
   - Discuss budget range
   - Explain SMB process
   - Determine if site visit needed

3. **Site Visit** (if applicable)
   - Assess site conditions
   - Discuss feasibility
   - Refine scope and budget
   - Build relationship

4. **Proposal**
   - Rough budget or detailed estimate (depending on phase)
   - Timeline
   - Process overview
   - Next steps

5. **Follow-Up**
   - Answer questions
   - Refine proposal if needed
   - Move toward contract

### Service-Specific Strategies

**Custom & Semi-Custom Homes**

- **Target**: Homeowners with land, budget for custom build
- **Pitch**: Mountain-modern design, reliable execution, single-team management
- **Differentiator**: Design-forward, schedule discipline, mountain expertise

**Spec & Investment Homes**

- **Target**: Investors, developers, JV partners
- **Pitch**: Proven floor plans, tight budgets, reliable schedules, JV options
- **Differentiator**: Vertical integration (Grid, Stax), margin efficiency

**Modular Installs (Stax)**

- **Target**: Homeowners interested in modular, builders needing install services
- **Pitch**: Factory efficiency + on-site craftsmanship, faster timeline
- **Differentiator**: Stax partnership, Grid cabinet integration, full-service install

**Remodels & Additions**

- **Target**: Existing homeowners, vacation property owners
- **Pitch**: Same planning discipline as new construction, mountain-modern aesthetic
- **Differentiator**: Grid cabinet integration, design-forward approach

### Pricing Strategy

- **Custom Homes**: Cost-plus or fixed-price (depending on project)
- **Spec Homes**: Competitive pricing, JV profit-share options
- **Modular Installs**: Transparent pricing, package deals with Stax modules
- **Remodels**: Time and materials or fixed-price (depending on scope)

### Key Messaging

- "Mountain-Modern. Built Right."
- Schedule discipline and budget protection
- Design-forward but buildable
- Single-team management (no GC/sub chaos)
- Mountain expertise (snow loads, access, utilities)

## Grid Cabinet Systems — Sales Strategy

### Target Customers

**Primary Buyers**

1. **Builders & GCs**
   - Production builders
   - Custom home builders
   - Remodel contractors
   - Small to mid-size operations

2. **Modular Manufacturers**
   - Stax (internal)
   - Other modular factories
   - Prefab manufacturers

3. **Remodelers**
   - Kitchen remodelers
   - Bath remodelers
   - General contractors doing remodels

4. **Retail Customers** (future potential)
   - Homeowners doing remodels
   - Designers specifying cabinets

### Sales Channels

**Direct Sales**

- Website inquiries
- Phone/email
- Builder outreach
- Trade shows
- Referrals

**Dealer/Wholesale Program** (future)

- Authorized dealers
- Volume pricing
- Marketing support

### Sales Process

**Inquiry → Quote → Order**

1. **Initial Contact**
   - Layout or plan provided
   - SKU list (if known)
   - Color selection
   - Timeline requirements

2. **Quote Process**
   - Review layout/plan
   - Translate to SKUs
   - Provide pricing
   - Estimate lead time
   - Identify QuickBuild™ eligibility

3. **Quote Presentation**
   - SKU list and quantities
   - Pricing breakdown
   - Lead time estimate
   - QuickBuild™ option (if applicable)
   - Payment terms

4. **Order Confirmation**
   - Buyer approves quote
   - Purchase order or signed agreement
   - Deposit paid
   - Order enters production queue

5. **Production & Delivery**
   - Regular updates (if requested)
   - Completion notification
   - Pickup or delivery coordination

### QuickBuild™ Program

**Marketing Pitch**

- "4–7 day cabinet packages"
- "Schedule protection"
- "No surprises"
- "Standard SKUs, fast turnaround"

**Eligibility**

- Standard SKUs only
- 5 core colors
- No custom modifications
- Sufficient lead time (not same-day rush)

**Sales Strategy**

- Highlight QuickBuild™ in all marketing
- Offer QuickBuild™ option on every quote
- Emphasize schedule protection
- Volume discounts for repeat QuickBuild™ customers

### Strategic Stock Reserve (SSR) as Sales Tool

**Messaging**

- "We keep material on hand to protect your schedule"
- "SSR enables QuickBuild™ program"
- "No material delays = no schedule slips"

**Sales Benefit**

- Differentiator vs. competitors
- Schedule reliability = customer retention
- Premium pricing potential

### SKU Catalog as Sales Tool

**Benefits**

- Easy for builders to specify
- Repeatable across projects
- Predictable pricing
- Quick quoting

**Sales Strategy**

- Provide catalog to all prospects
- Help builders design to SKU catalog
- Show how SKUs work across multiple projects
- Emphasize repeatability and efficiency

### Pricing Strategy

- **Standard Jobs**: Competitive pricing, target margin
- **QuickBuild™**: Premium pricing potential (speed = value)
- **Volume**: Volume discounts for repeat customers
- **Internal (Stax/SMB)**: Transfer pricing (at cost or slight margin)

### Key Messaging

- "System-Built. CNC-Precise."
- "Cabinetry That Doesn't Miss Schedules"
- Fast turnaround (QuickBuild™)
- Predictable pricing
- Standardized SKUs
- Schedule protection (SSR)

### Builder Partnerships

**200-Unit Builder Strategy**

- Volume pricing
- Guaranteed production slots
- Dedicated account management
- Custom SKU programs (if needed)
- Long-term agreements

**Primary Buyers**

1. **Homeowners**
   - ADU seekers
   - Small home buyers
   - Cabin/vacation home buyers
   - Modern design enthusiasts

2. **Builders & GCs**
   - Builders wanting modular product
   - GCs offering modular to clients
   - Production builders
   - Small to mid-size operations

3. **Developers**
   - Small community developers
   - Resort developers
   - Infill developers
   - Workforce housing developers

4. **Investors**
   - Spec home investors
   - Rental property investors
   - JV partners

### Sales Channels

**Direct Sales (Homeowners)**

- Website inquiries
- Referrals from SMB
- Social media
- Home shows
- ADU-specific marketing

**Builder Sales**

- Builder outreach
- Trade shows
- Referrals
- Partnership programs

**Developer Sales**

- Direct outreach
- RFP responses
- Partnership proposals
- JV opportunities

### Sales Process (Homeowners)

**Inquiry → Site Assessment → Quote → Order**

1. **Initial Contact**
   - Website form, phone, email
   - Understand needs (ADU, primary home, cabin, etc.)
   - Budget range
   - Location/site

2. **Site Assessment**
   - Site visit (if local) or remote assessment
   - Access evaluation
   - Foundation requirements
   - Utility availability
   - Zoning/permits

3. **Model & Options Selection**
   - Choose Stax model (Scout, Ridge, Summit, etc.)
   - Select options package (Quartz, Garnet, Sapphire)
   - Customizations (if any)

4. **Quote**
   - Module pricing
   - Options pricing
   - Transport cost (if applicable)
   - Installation cost (via SMB or partner GC)
   - Total project cost

5. **Order**
   - Deposit
   - Production schedule
   - Installation coordination

### Sales Process (Builders)

**Inquiry → Partnership Discussion → Volume Agreement**

1. **Initial Contact**
   - Builder outreach or inbound inquiry
   - Understand builder's needs
   - Volume potential

2. **Product Presentation**
   - Stax model lineup
   - Floorplans and specs
   - Options packages
   - Pricing structure

3. **Partnership Discussion**
   - Volume pricing
   - Production slot guarantees
   - Installation support
   - Marketing support

4. **Agreement**
   - Volume purchase agreement
   - Pricing structure
   - Production schedule
   - Terms and conditions

### Sales Process (Developers)

**Inquiry → Proposal → JV Discussion**

1. **Initial Contact**
   - Developer outreach or inbound inquiry
   - Understand project scope
   - Volume and timeline

2. **Proposal**
   - Stax product fit
   - Pricing (volume discounts)

3. **JV Discussion** (if applicable)
   - JV structure (Valhalla/SMB + Developer)
   - Profit share
   - Responsibilities
   - Timeline

4. **Agreement**
   - Purchase agreement or JV agreement
   - Production schedule
   - Terms and conditions
