# Startup Costs Model

**Note**: This document outlines cost categories and structure. Specific dollar amounts should be filled in based on local market conditions, quotes, and actual project requirements.

## Grid Cabinet Systems — Startup Costs

### Phase 1: Leased Shop (Initial Launch)

**Equipment**
- CNC router (5×10 or 4×8)
- Edgebander
- Table saw / panel saw
- Drill press / boring machine
- Assembly tables
- Material handling equipment
- Dust collection system

**Initial Inventory**
- Melamine sheet goods (5-color core line)
- Edgebanding material
- Hardware (hinges, slides, pulls)
- Fasteners and assembly materials
- Strategic Stock Reserve (SSR) initial stock

**Shop Setup**
- Lease deposit / first month
- Utilities hookup
- Compressed air system
- Material storage racks
- Office setup (minimal)

**Working Capital**
- Operating expenses (3–6 months)
- Payroll buffer
- Marketing / initial sales

**Soft Costs**
- Business formation
- Insurance
- Permits / licenses
- Initial marketing materials

---

### Phase 3: Owned Facility (40×100 Shop)

**Land Acquisition** (via Valhalla)
- 3–5 acres, light industrial zoned
- Site selection and due diligence

**Building Construction**
- 40×100 shop structure
- Office space
- Material yard / covered storage
- Parking and access roads
- Utilities (power, water, septic/sewer)
- Compressed air infrastructure

**Site Development**
- Grading and site prep
- Driveway / access
- Utility connections
- Drainage

**Equipment** (same as Phase 1, plus expansion capacity)

**Lease-to-Own Structure**
- Valhalla owns building
- Grid leases with purchase option
- Terms to be defined

## Stax Modular — Startup Costs

### Phase 4: Prototype Yard

**Site Development** (on Valhalla-owned land)
- 2–3 covered production bays
- Crane-friendly layout
- Dry box storage area
- Power and compressed air
- Transport staging area
- Basic material storage

**Initial Equipment**
- Overhead crane or gantry
- Basic assembly jigs
- Material handling
- Tools for floor/wall/roof assembly

**Prototype Materials**
- First 2–4 module builds
- Windows, doors, roofing
- MEP rough-in materials
- Grid cabinets (internal cost)

**Working Capital**
- Initial payroll
- Material purchases
- Transport coordination

---

### Phase 5: Full Modular Factory

**Building Construction**
- 12,000–20,000 sq ft facility
- High-bay ceiling for overhead crane
- Multiple production lines
- QC stations
- Dock doors for transport
- Office and break areas

**Production Equipment**
- Overhead line system
- Travel lifts
- Auto sprayers (if applicable)
- Assembly jigs (multiple stations)
- Material handling systems
- QC equipment

**Site Infrastructure**
- Expanded yard for module storage
- Transport coordination area
- Material receiving
- Waste management

**Working Capital**
- Larger material inventory
- Payroll for production team
- Transport partnerships
- Marketing / builder outreach

## Valhalla Legacy Group — Startup Costs

**Target Parcels**
