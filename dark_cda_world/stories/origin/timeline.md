# Origin – Timeline

Rough chronological order of key events in the Origin era.

1. Family life before Trader arrives
2. Trader (Arpin) arrives with new goods and ideas (six months before Chapter 1)
3. Coin and pouch become separated from their original owner
4. Early imbalances (sickness, bad luck, strange weather)
5. Sela's gift begins to show
6. **Chapter 1: Circles in the Pines** - Taman and Sela track elk in circles; Isaac uses coin to resolve dispute; Arpin's ledger system in place; settlement under strain
7. **Chapter 2: Fort Shadow** - Daily rhythm of settlement; flour dispute with Tom and Ruth; Boone explains ledgers as "map to safety"; missionary appears warning of tremors and weight; small ground shake (Arpin calls it "settling")
8. **Chapter 3: Tracks and Ledgers** - Taman tracks sign that won't commit; Arpin forgives Mary's debt (creating obligation); Isaac's coin influence; Boone talks future plans (roads, routes, wagons); Taman dreams of mountain with unmoving cloud
9. **Chapter 4: The Spring That Remembers** - Taman hikes to high spring; reflection stutters showing something split/older; hawk perches close, counting him; Coyote appears; coin flip (Taman calls heads, wins, but realizes he chose to engage); spring knows Taman by weight
10. **Chapter 5: The First Stop** - Taman proposes leaving east; Boone refuses (bound to ledgers/future); Arpin blocks Taman's departure (debts/obligations in ledger); severe storm rolls in; ash-scented wind when sun returns (no fire source)
11. **Chapter 6: The Weight of Names** - Taman and Sela discuss weight and choices; Sela says weight follows you, part of what you carry; discussion of names having weight; Arpin uses names with calculated kindness to create obligation; scales tipping, weight building
12. **Chapter 7: First Blood** - Hiram the blacksmith speaks openly against Arpin; Arpin strikes him with backhand and iron rod (first open violence); Isaac reveals coin publicly, dazzles crowd with "order"; Taman wants to help but Boone stops him; missionary mutters of crooked scales; larger tremor shakes the fort
13. **Chapter 8: Boone's Silence** - Taman presses Boone to stand against Arpin; Boone refuses, insists playing along protects family; Taman seeks guidance in missionary's chapel; missionary speaks in riddles (names weigh more than sins, even Christ stumbled); Isaac's coin silences missionary's voice; Taman realizes no god will fight this battle
14. **Chapter 9: The Charity Ledger** - Arpin announces "gift" of food and tools; Isaac flashes coin, people cheer and sign ledger pages; Taman sees gifts as chains; Caleb argues for water storage, Arpin mocks him, Silas drags him away; hawk dives low over square; fissure opens near spring with warm mist rising
15. **Chapter 10: Ash on the Wind** - Rumors of sick animals (wolves with foamed mouths, elk dropping); Arpin dismisses it, men seize carcasses; Sela warns weight can twist animals and land; thin layer of ash dusts roofs with no fire source; missionary's warning taken seriously for once
  - **Act II Complete** (Chapters 6-10: Arpin Ascendant)
16. **Chapter 11: The Cracks Deepen** - Larger tremor at dawn (buckets tilt, lantern falls); Arpin dismisses as "nerves"; Boone preaches patience, claims ledgers will free them; Sela urges caution—"not yet"; hawk drops feather at Taman's feet; Taman dreams of road leading out of valley
17. **Chapter 12: The False Guide Returns** - Taman seeks guidance at missionary's chapel after Boone's silence; missionary raves about scales and sin, claims "Christ's cross was no lighter than your sister's"; missionary offers wagon bound for east fort with men who will listen; Isaac's coin silences missionary's voice mid-verse when he passes; Taman seizes chance to go east, ignoring riddles
18. **Chapter 13: The Road East** - Taman slips away at dawn with missionary's wagon, Sela's blessing like warmth in his chest; road east steadier, tremors fading, birdsong without echo; meets mother's tribe at river crossing; elder explains balance is sharing weight, not absence, warns Sela's gift will draw hunters; Taman meets Mira (trader's daughter); experiences balanced scales at tribal campfire; sleeps without dreams but knows calm is temporary
19. **Chapter 14: The Other Fort** - Eastern fort larger than Fort Shadow, marked with welcome signs, humming with balance; healthy animals, laughing children, no ash in air; council hears Taman's plea, one trader offers quiet supply route west; Mira warns Arpin's grip won't weaken, urges Taman to bring family east; mother's tribe says they won't aid Arpin or raise hand against him, their balance is their own; elder offers Taman place to stay but he refuses, must return to save family; leaves with provisions, map, bitter knowledge he tasted balance and walked away
20. **Chapter 15: A Fragile Win** - First supply wagons arrive quietly from east, loads small but honest without ledgers; Boone warns Arpin will see it as theft; townsfolk whisper with hope; Sela warns "scales never stop moving"; Isaac rubs coin harder, warns Arpin something slipping; Arpin orders Isaac to find who's challenging control; larger tremor rattles crockery, missionary laughs; Taman realizes he's won sliver of ground but painted target on all their backs
  - **Act III Complete** (Chapters 11-15: First Counter)
21. **Chapter 16: The Road Not Taken** - Taman returns urging family east, tells of balance and plenty; Boone refuses, insists fort is home, Arpin's grip survivable; mother listens in silence shadowed by tribe's warnings; Sela won't abandon Boone without consent; Isaac rubs coin, whispers duty/inheritance to Boone; missionary babbles "all roads lead to scales"; tremor knocks apples, people laugh it off; Taman feels east slipping away
  - **Act IV Begins** (Chapters 16-20: Beating & Betrayal)
22. **Chapter 17: Ledger Chains** - Arpin unveils new terms binding every family in debt so deep no one could leave; Isaac flashes coin in sun, turns suspicion into applause; Taman tries to rally neighbors but debts gag their courage; Sela warns open defiance brings weight down harder; Boone sides with silence, mutters "numbers don't lie"; missionary whispers "truth is in the weight, not the word"; Taman dreams of east fort, wakes to walls pressing close; hawk's cry splits morning
23. **Chapter 18: The Beating** - Mother speaks against Arpin in trading square, names his rule cruelty; Arpin steps down, strikes her hard, blows falling heavy until she staggers in dirt; Isaac rubs coin, crowd's anger ebbs into uneasy silence; Taman lunges but Sela clamps around him; Boone stands rooted, pale and silent, ledger like shield; Arpin sneers threat about debt and flesh; missionary mutters "wages of sin is weight"; tremor splits crock that night, Taman feels scales groaning
  - **Act IV continues** (Chapters 16-20: Beating & Betrayal)
24. **Chapter 19: The Wavering Brother** - Boone begins to waver, haunted by cowardice; Isaac catches hesitation, manipulates with coin; Sela presses Boone but his silence cuts deeper; missionary mutters "names weigh more than numbers"; Taman tries to lead family east again but guards turn them back; Arpin grins at humiliation; Boone dreams of mother's blood, whispers "maybe we should try again—but not yet"
25. **Chapter 20: Groaning Earth** - Supplies dwindle, Arpin hoards; Isaac rubs coin so often even children notice; Boone finally admits Arpin must be stopped (halting, fragile); missionary staggers into square shouting mountain is listening; another tremor shakes fort stronger than ever; animals panic, sickened mule drops dead; Sela's gift can't revive it; earth groans, people of Fort Shadow hush—for first time they hear what Taman hears
  - **Act IV Complete** (Chapters 16-20: Beating & Betrayal)
  - **Act V Begins** (Chapters 21-25: Toll of Silence)
26. **Chapter 21: Boone's Fracture** - Boone cannot sleep, haunted by mother's bruises; confesses to Taman Arpin may have gone too far (fear still knots tongue); Sela urges leaving for east but Isaac whispers to Arpin about "traitors"; Boone drifts through fort like ghost, listens to numbers instead of conscience; Taman presses "Blood's heavier than ink"; missionary appears "names weigh more than debts"; hawk's cry slices dawn, Boone startles; for first time Boone looks at Arpin with doubt instead of awe
27. **Chapter 22: Arpin's Toll** - Arpin gathers fort, railing about betrayal; singles Boone out, forces confession of divided loyalties; Boone stammers, unable to lie, silence is punishment; Arpin exacts toll in humiliation—Boone made public clerk writing debts under mother's bruised eye; Isaac rubs coin, people cheer spectacle; Taman burns with shame, vowing Boone won't be left in chains; missionary cackles "pound of flesh, pound of ink"; wolf found dead inside fort walls, foam on lips
28. **Chapter 23: The Cavalry Rumor** - Word comes cavalry may ride, men ready to break Arpin's hold; fort buzzes with hope, Taman seizes on it; Arpin plays smooth, welcomes rumor; behind closed doors prepares gold to turn soldiers into mercenaries; Boone writes terms sick with shame; missionary mutters of armies marching in circles; hawk perches on wall watching drills; Taman feels ground tremor—rhythm of drums beneath soil
29. **Chapter 24: The Mercenary Turn** - Cavalry arrives as Arpin's newest weapon, bought by silver and Isaac's coin; people cheer until they see whose flag they march under; Boone watches soldiers drink in Arpin's hall, bile rising; Taman whispers balance tilting past saving; Arpin parades through square with soldiers; missionary shuffles past laughing; tremor shakes loose stones from wall; Taman feels mountain groaning like it has soldiers within
30. **Chapter 25: Signs of Rot** - Animals fall sick in pens, tongues swelling, eyes clouding, deaths quick and ugly; Arpin blames Sela, shouting her weight poisons fort; Isaac rubs coin, people nod; Boone protests softly, Arpin silences with glance; missionary kneels by dead calf whispering "even beasts bear weight"; hawk drifts overhead but does not cry; Sela clings to mother who insists sickness not her fault but voice trembles; that night ash falls on fort though no fire burns
  - **Act V Complete** (Chapters 21-25: Toll of Silence)
  - **Act VI Begins** (Chapters 26-30: The Trap Tightens)
31. **Chapter 26: The Knock at Night** - Fort grows tense, whispers about Sela's weight spread like wildfire; Arpin fans flames, says mountain's anger is her fault; at night men break into Taman's cabin, seize Mother while Sela flees into dark; Boone watches but does nothing; Arpin parades Mother as proof he will "root out the curse"; Taman tries to rally help but townsfolk avert eyes; missionary mutters of lambs led to slaughter; Taman realizes only he knows where Sela has hidden—burden bending him
32. **Chapter 27: Pressure from All Sides** - Arpin interrogates Taman publicly demanding Sela's whereabouts; Isaac whispers stroking coin; townsfolk surround Taman begging him to "do the right thing"; Mother beaten in front of him, cries twisting resolve; Sela sends hidden sign from hiding place trusting him; Boone avoids his eyes; hawk circles above screeching; Taman cracks, says he will bring Sela in if Mother is freed
33. **Chapter 28: The Bargain** - Taman leads Arpin's posse toward Sela's hiding place; silently prays for miracle, missionary's nonsense rattling; Isaac toys with him speaking of balance and cost of secrets; they arrive but Sela has already moved on—leaving only her pouch as sign; Arpin rages, calls Taman liar, strikes Mother again; Isaac smooths it over promising fate always claims toll; earth trembles; Taman sees scales tipping in real time, betrayal wasted
34. **Chapter 29: The Collapse of Trust** - Sela slips deeper into woods carrying weight of her gift and Taman's weakness; Taman returns to Shadow as pariah, townsfolk hiss "traitor"; Boone confronts him with fury: "You sold her out, and for what?"; Taman's words fall flat; Mother still not released—Arpin claims Sela's capture only price; missionary speaks of Judas and silver; Isaac's coin gleams mocking; alone in night Taman flees into woods chasing shadows of Sela and Coyote
35. **Chapter 30: Into the Woods** - Taman wanders among trees whispering with wind, mountain groaning louder; guilt gnaws—betrayed one sister, failed to save other; fire smolders in distance (one of Seven), ash drifting; hears mother's voice echo in trees—not real but heavy as judgment; hawk follows as sentinel; Coyote appears offering games instead of answers; "You've already played once, boy. Shall we try again?"; Taman realizes standing in thinnest place yet—balance ready to break
  - **Act VI Complete** (Chapters 26-30: The Trap Tightens)
  - **Act VII Begins** (Chapters 31-35: The Last Chase)
36. **Chapter 31: Shackled Promises** - Sela remains in hiding, slipping deeper into woods, pouch clutched tight; Taman skulks forest's edge tormented by visions of Mother in chains; Arpin parades through Shadow demanding Sela, building frenzy; Boone torn seeing Sela as sister but feeling powerless; Isaac rubs coin whispering "curse" must be burned; missionary mutters half-Bible half-gibberish; hawk hovers tracking both; at night Arpin drags Mother from cell—final cruelty beginning
37. **Chapter 32: The Pound of Flesh** - Isaac watches Arpin take "pound of flesh" from Mother in silence, whispers "balance" as if absolving; Arpin cradles Mother's head telling her pain has been paid; her last words curse him—balance will find him—and he kills her gently claiming mercy; Boone hears but does nothing, cowardice hardening into weight; Arpin tells town debt settled but Isaac steps forward coin glowing, insists mountain keeps cracking until Sela's blood feeds scales; Arpin declares Sela's death balance due, whips crowd toward frenzy; trio flank him: Kerrick with axe, Silas looping rope, Marlowe sharpening knives; mountain groans audibly through squareboards
38. **Chapter 33: The Eastward Plea** - Taman flees to east fort desperate for soldiers or allies; east fort thrives but commander refuses to meddle in Shadow's self-made storm; Taman begs missionary who rambles of lambs, Judas, debts unpaid; Boone arrives secretly telling Taman: Mother is gone, Sela will be taken within days; Taman collapses, betrayal echoing Judas' thrice denial, hawk's shadow crosses his hands; tribe listens but holds balance—will neither aid Arpin nor take up arms; mercenaries already lean Arpin's way; Taman turns back anyway unwilling to leave Sela to knife
39. **Chapter 34: The Net Closes** - Arpin announces Sela must still be captured: "the curse lingers until the mountain sleeps"; his men scour woods, pressing Boone to lead them; trio prowl edges; Isaac whispers "Family betrays family. It is the oldest law" and Boone breaks, hinting at Sela's trail; Arpin leads posse himself, coin thrumming, villagers whipped into frenzy; Sela evades through gullies and underbrush but running out of ground; Taman rushes back too late—torches weave through trees like net; Hiram, Marta, Caleb try to misdirect but shoved aside; for first time mountain groans loud enough to knock stones from slopes
40. **Chapter 35: The Breaking Point** - Trio corner Sela and drag her back in chains; Boone watches tears streaking face, complicity a wound that won't close; Arpin declares balance must be restored—Sela's sacrifice will seal it—town half-hypnotized nods; Isaac whispers scripture twisted into law, coin hot, even mercenaries lower eyes; Coyote mocks Taman for trusting missionary's babble; missionary howls garbled verse; Taman reaches Shadow's edge breathless and bloodied too late again; Sela calls his name not in fear but in forgiveness—breaks him deeper than any blow; ground cracks beneath fort—mountain's true answer gathering in throat
  - **Act VII Complete** (Chapters 31-35: The Last Chase)
  - **Act VIII Begins** (Chapters 36-40: Palaver & Eruption)
41. **Chapter 36: The Palaver Begins** - Taman flees deep into woods half-mad with grief and guilt; mountain's groans sound like voices now—low, hollow, accusing; hawk follows still, cry sharp against silence; in thin place by spring Coyote appears grinning, tail flicking; "You've played this game before, boy. Shall we play again?"; Taman demands mother back, demands Sela's freedom—Coyote laughs; Isaac's whispers echo as though coin carries across worlds; first round of their game begins—simple odds, yet neither side wins
42. **Chapter 37: The Graybreaker's Shadow** - Ritual to kill Sela prepared in Shadow: torches, drums, frenzy; Isaac tells Arpin this act will cement him—the "King of Balance"; Arpin drunk on power parades Sela before people like prize; Boone breaks down whispering apologies no one hears; Hiram, Marta, Caleb try in small ways to resist but silenced; Kerrick, Silas, Marlowe flank Arpin, town's sins given muscle; in woods Taman loses again and again in Coyote's games, blood dripping from bitten tongue; mountain shakes splitting stones; sky burns unnatural red
43. **Chapter 38: The Final Wager** - Coyote offers Taman last game: coin toss, simple, pure chance; Taman hesitates—knows chance is balance but desperation blinds him; he calls it, loses, rages, blood choking mouth; Coyote tilts head amused: "Balance always wins. You only speed it along"; Taman feels something break—not outside but inside; hawk wheels overhead then vanishes into smoke rising from mountain; back at Shadow ritual begins, Isaac nearly trembling with ecstasy; Arpin raises knife to Sela's throat as ground buckles
44. **Chapter 39: The Mountain Pops** - Eruption tears sky, black smoke blotting out stars; fort shudders, walls crack and tumble; lava and ash roar down slope like living beast; Kerrick, Silas, Marlowe fight chaos but cruelty cannot stop stone and fire; Hiram, Marta, Caleb swept aside—virtue crushed alongside sin; Arpin screaming clutches Isaac's coin as heat consumes him; Isaac laughs even as body burns, promising he will return; Taman sees only red and gray as world splits—his body falling one way, his name the other
45. **Chapter 40: The Split** - Taman awakens in thin place, alone, pouch clutched tight; his name catches in throat—bitten off, unsaid; on other side another form rises, empty, grinning—Walter born; Coyote sits on spring's edge watching both with sly amusement; "One became two," he says. "Balance shifts. The game goes on"; hawk returns circling once before vanishing into mirror-sky; Shadow buried under ash and stone, erased as if never was; tale closes not with triumph but with silence—the birth of the Gray-Touched
